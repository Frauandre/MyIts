Mammals are a class of homeotherm vertebrates, warm-blooded animals, that is they are able to maintain a constant body temperature. The main characteristic of vertebrates is the presence in females of mammary glands that allow them to feed their young with their mother's milk, the number varies from species to species. The body of mammals is covered by hairs, in some cases they form a thick fur and in other cases they are very reduced or collected, whereas marine mammals are completely devoid of it

The circulatory system, with double and complete circulation, consists of the heart with four cavities by veins and arteries. The nervous system presents a great development of the encephalon with large cerebral hemispheres.
